The Desk: Perfect Match

Welcome to your internship at the Career Advising Center! Your task is simple: review local everyday job openings and determine if the student's resume is an appropriate match to send over for an interview.

How to Analyze the Papers:

  • Step 1: Look at the **Job Description** (on the left clipboard) to see what skills, tools, or schedule they are asking for.
  • Step 2: Check the **Student's Resume** (on the right pad). Do they have transferable skills? Are they available during those hours?
  • Step 3: Make your decision: **Yes (Match)** or **No (Mismatch)**. Read the coaching tips to master resume writing!

Classroom Resume Key Lessons:

  • Check Your Schedule First: If a job description lists explicit shift requirements (like Saturday mornings), a candidate's availability *must* match. No alignment means immediate rejection.
  • Highlight Transferable Skills: Babysitting counts as time management. Fast food cash registers count as customer service. Reframe your everyday experience!
  • Customize Your Keywords: Don't use a generic resume for every position. If the desk asks for Canva or Microsoft Office, those exact keywords should be prominently visible on the resume page.
